Market Structure

Market Structure is the basic framework of how price moves — the most fundamental concept in technical analysis. Markets rarely move in a straight line; instead, they move in a series of zig-zags forming waves.

Uptrends are characterized by a series of Higher Highs (HH) and Higher Lows (HL). This indicates that buyers are in control and are willing to pay higher prices.

Downtrends consist of Lower Lows (LL) and Lower Highs (LH). Here, sellers dominate, pushing prices further down.

A market is in consolidation (ranging) when there are no clear HH/HL or LL/LH sequences — price bounces between defined support and resistance levels. Avoid trading ranges until a clear breakout occurs.

Key Takeaway Never trade against the prevailing higher-timeframe market structure. "The trend is your friend until it bends."
Candlestick Patterns

Japanese candlesticks provide a visual representation of price action during a specific timeframe. Every candle tells a story about the battle between buyers (bulls) and sellers (bears).

The Doji

A Doji occurs when open and close prices are virtually identical. It represents market indecision and a potential turning point if found at major support/resistance levels.

Hammer & Shooting Star

Characterized by a small body and a long lower (hammer) or upper (shooting star) wick. They show a strong rejection of price levels, hinting at a reversal.

Engulfing Pattern

A two-candle pattern where the second candle's real body completely 'engulfs' the previous one. A bullish engulfing at a support zone is a powerful buy signal.

Risk Management

You can have the best trading strategy in the world, but without proper risk management, you will eventually blow your account. Your primary job as a trader is capital preservation.

The 1-2% Rule

Never risk more than 1%–2% of your total account equity on a single trade. With a $10,000 account, your max risk should be $100–$200.

Risk:Reward Ratio

Always aim for trades where the potential reward outweighs the risk. A standard minimum is 1:2 R:R. You only need to win 34% of trades to break even.

Position Sizing

Your lot size should be calculated mathematically before every trade. Use the Trading Plan Tool on this dashboard to determine your exact position size.

Drawdown Management

Set a daily loss limit (e.g., 3%) and a weekly loss limit (e.g., 6%). If you hit these limits, stop trading for that period.
